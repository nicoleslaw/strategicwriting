## Editing

Writing is a first draft, editing is the rest of the work
I think of the line between writing and editing as being pretty blurry

Biggest tip for editing if you can‚Äôt show your work to somebody: read your work aloud
Helps catch sentences that are clumsy or too long
Immediately makes it more conversational
Main takeaway from this class: if you‚Äôre not sure how to write it, say it out loud and record it, and write that
Write things like you would say them in conversation

When reading aloud, think about:
* Is it clear?
* Is it true?
* Is it useful?
* Does it sound like a nice person said it? (vs a robot)

### Collaborate

Important thing: see each other as a resource. You don‚Äôt have to be a writer to know if something is confusing. You don‚Äôt have to be a designer to know that a design is bad. You don‚Äôt need to think of yourself as an editor to edit people‚Äôs work.

* Very easy (even for nicole!) to get lost in what you want to say vs what‚Äôs actually coming out

### Exercise time!

* Find a partner and swap pieces with them.
* Read their piece, and then read it again slowly.
*  Don‚Äôt start fixing typos or editing while you‚Äôre reading. Read the whole thing once through, slowly, then read again and edit. Start high-level, make sure the piece is doing what it is trying to do, then get into the spelling, grammar, etc.

Ask yourself:
* Does it make sense? Does it flow?
* Is it useful?
* Is it interesting?
* Are there any gaps or places where you could use an example?

I encourage you to use your peers in this way as much as you can! Builds trust, makes both your work stronger.
